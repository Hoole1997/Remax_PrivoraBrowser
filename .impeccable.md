## Design Context

### Users

Privora Browser serves Android users across gesture-navigation and traditional button-navigation devices, including lower-end phones. Users expect core browsing, file, cleaning, and media flows to remain responsive and for primary controls to stay clear of system UI.

### Brand Personality

Preserve the established interface rather than introducing a new visual direction. The product should feel simple, practical, and dependable.

### Aesthetic Direction

Keep the existing light surfaces, blue primary actions, rounded controls, and concise utility-oriented presentation. Support the current multilingual UI and do not restyle existing screens unless explicitly requested.

### Design Principles

- Keep important content and touch targets outside system bars, cutouts, and gesture areas.
- Adapt from runtime WindowInsets instead of fixed device-specific dimensions.
- Preserve existing visual hierarchy and business behavior when making compatibility fixes.
- Prefer small, reusable UI infrastructure over screen-specific padding patches.
- Prioritize stable behavior and low runtime overhead on lower-end Android devices.
